# Introduction to Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics\n\n## Course Overview\n\nThis textbook provides a comprehensive introduction to Physical Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Humanoid Robotics. It is designed to equip students with the foundational knowledge and practical skills necessary to understand, design, and implement intelligent robotic systems. The curriculum covers core concepts from basic robot kinematics and dynamics to advanced topics in embodied intelligence, ROS 2 (Robot Operating System 2), sensor fusion, and real-world robotics applications. Through a blend of theoretical explanations, hands-on coding examples, and practical exercises, students will gain a holistic understanding of how AI principles are applied in the physical domain.\n\n## Learning Outcomes\n\nUpon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:\n\n1.  **Understand the fundamentals of Physical AI**: Differentiate between Physical AI and Digital AI, and comprehend the unique challenges and opportunities in embodied intelligence.\n2.  **Master ROS 2 Basics**: Navigate the ROS 2 ecosystem, understand nodes, topics, services, actions, and create basic robotic applications.\n3.  **Implement Sensor Systems**: Explain various sensor technologies (LIDAR, cameras, IMU) and apply sensor fusion techniques for robust perception.\n4.  **Develop Robot Control Algorithms**: Design and implement algorithms for robot motion planning, inverse kinematics, and task execution.\n5.  **Utilize Simulation Environments**: Work with Gazebo and other simulation tools to test and validate robotic systems before hardware deployment.\n6.  **Analyze Real-world Applications**: Explore case studies of humanoid robots and Physical AI in industrial, service, and research domains.\n7.  **Apply Best Practices**: Understand principles of robot safety, ethical considerations, and efficient software development for robotics.\n\n## Target Audience\n\nThis textbook is intended for:\n\n*   Undergraduate and graduate students in Robotics, Computer Science, Electrical Engineering, and Mechanical Engineering.\n*   Researchers and professionals transitioning into Physical AI or Humanoid Robotics.\n*   Hobbyists with a strong technical background interested in building intelligent robots.\n\n## Prerequisites\n\nTo get the most out of this textbook, students should have:\n\n*   **Programming Skills**: Intermediate proficiency in Python (including object-oriented programming concepts).\n*   **Mathematics**: Basic understanding of linear algebra, calculus, and probability.\n*   **Operating System**: Familiarity with Linux command-line interface (Ubuntu preferred).\n*   **Robotics Concepts**: Basic understanding of robot kinematics and control theory (beneficial but not strictly required).\n
